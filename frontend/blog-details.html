Cut AWS costs with Reserved Instances and Savings Plans

On-demand pricing is convenient, but paying full rate for workloads that run around the clock is one of the most common sources of cloud waste. Reserved Instances and Savings Plans let you trade a usage or spend commitment for discounts of up to seventy percent. The challenge is not whether to commit, but how much to commit, for how long, and to which services without leaving capacity stranded.

The first step is understanding your baseline. Look at the portion of your compute that runs consistently every hour of every day, month after month. That steady-state usage is the safest candidate for a commitment, because you are confident it will still be there when the term ends. Bursty, seasonal, or experimental workloads should stay on demand until their patterns stabilize, so you avoid paying for reservations you cannot use.

The goal of a commitment strategy is not maximum coverage, it is maximum coverage you are confident you will actually consume. Over-committing simply moves waste from on-demand spend into unused reservations.

Let CloudPi recommend the right coverage automatically

Modeling commitments by hand across AWS, Azure, and GCP quickly becomes unmanageable as your footprint grows. CloudPi continuously analyzes your usage history, blends Savings Plans with Reserved Instances, and recommends the optimal coverage level and term for each workload. It also tracks utilization after you commit, alerts you when coverage drifts, and surfaces opportunities to extend or rebalance, so your savings compound instead of eroding over time.
